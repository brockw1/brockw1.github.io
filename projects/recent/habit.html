HABIT VR

HABIT VR is an award winning Senior Design project that I worked on for my Computer Science degree. I worked with 9 other undergraduate students as a developer to create a virtual reality videogame for the University of Nebraska Medical Center - Munroe-Meyer Institute. This game is meant to aid in physical therapy for children with cerebral palsy. This is a year long project and is still in progress. It is being made in Unity with SteamVR.

We were the recipient of the Gold Team Award in the Senior Design program. You can view our project and the other projects on thier website: https://cse.unl.edu/senior-design

The premise of our game is the player is a secret agent in training and is tasked with chasing down the nefarious Dr. Sphinx, who has set up a series of puzzles that the player must overcome. These puzzle/mini-games put a focus on different motions and motor functions, as well as forcing the player to use both hands. We have incorporated a 'Therapist UI' for each game that allows the therapist to control various difficulty and accesibility options when someone is playing the game. The game has a random-dungeon style design, allowing for replayability and a unique experience for each playthrough.

I was in charge of a lot of the level design, narrative, and art direction for this game. I worked with my team to make sure we had a clear vision for the game. I designed the layout of our tutorial area named the Training Center as well as modeled and animated several assets for the game, including Dr. Sphinx himself. I was also in charge of the narrative aspects of the game, so I wrote, recorded, and voice acted for most of the story aspects of the game. I also created several audio assets to aid in storytelling as well. Lastly, I designed and implemented two puzzles in the game called Ball Roll and Flashlight Searcher which incorporated different upper-body motions and challenges.
